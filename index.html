WELCOME!!!!

Are you ready to find the PHX that works best to give life to your tastebuds once again? So, you're probably curious why there is a recipe "foodie" site that has the mythical bird called the Phoenix all over its homepage? Let me clear this up right away! When I approach life in general, I can easily relate to The Phoenix because I too know what it feels like to rebuild and be reborn out the ashes of a previous life that burst into flames eventually.

This makes me approach anything that seems old or overdone or even just something that feels boring and try and build something great out the remnants of the original idea or project. This brings up the answer to the recipes and the idea for the entire site. This site is like The Phoenix because it is taking old, traditional, and boring recipes and basically burning the recipes and only keeping the best parts.

Then, once I put my touch on the recipes (or the chef, blogger, regular human being put their touch on the recipes), the recipe can be seen as having been born from the ashes of the original recipe. These fixed recipes then gave me the ultimate idea. The Phoenix can be abbreviated to PHX, which can be pronounced "fix"!

There you have it, I am Mr. PHXit and this is my Foodie PHXes Web site where the old and boring are being reborn into the new and trendy! Enjoy and I hope you do try out some of the recipes here! If you have an idea, there is a form to submit your own recipe(s) on this homepage in the lower right part of the page. I encourage you to send me your recipes because I may just try them out and then feature them (and you) on the site in the near future! Bon Appetit! Manga! Eat Up and Enjoy!!!!

S'MORES CAKE IN A JAR

Ingredients for the Crust
Number Measurement Ingredient
1 1½ cups graham cracker crumbs
2 ½ stick butter
3 a pinch salt

Directions for the Crust
Step Crust Directions
1 Preheat the Oven to 350°F.
2 Melt butter and mix in graham cracker crumbs and salt. Mix until moistened. Spray 4 mason jars with non-stick spray and press graham crust into jars. Start by adding a Tablespoon to each jar, and repeat that until graham crumbs are gone. They do not need to look perfect. I pressed the crumbs down firmly with the flat bottom of a clean screwdriver.

Ingredients for the Cake
Number Measurement Ingredient
1 1 1/8; cups All Purpose Flour
2 ¼ cup Dark cocoa powder
3 1¼ tsp baking soda
4 ½ tsp salt
5 ¾ cup brown sugar
6 1 qty egg
7 1 tsp vanilla extract
8 ½ cup Milk
9 ½ cup + 1 tbsp heavy cream
10 ½ cup butter, melted
11 2 tbsp sour cream
12 1 bag Large Marshmallows (for the topping)

Directions for the Cake
Step Cake Directions
1 In a bowl, whisk egg and sugar until smooth and no lumps remain. Add milk, cream, butter and vanilla, and mix until combined. Stir in sour cream. Sift dry ingredients together and add to wet mixture. Mix until batter is smooth. Using a 1/4 cup measure, add batter to mason jars one scoop at a time. You only want to fill them up about half way, and the batter should work evenly for 4 jars. Again – don’t worry if it is perfect. Place mason jars in a baking dish and add about 1 1/2 cups of water to the bottom. Bake for 30 minutes, or until cake is set.
2 Remove cake from the oven and press large marshmallows down on top, being careful not to burn yourself. There is no set number of marshmallows to use, I pressed down as many as I could, about 4-5 each. Heat the broiler on your oven and watching carefully, brown marshmallows for about 1-2 minutes, or until golden brown. Do not take your eyes off them! Serve immediately.
3 The recipe looks really long, but it is actually really easy! *When you put the marshmallows on top and broil, they may sink down below the edges of the chocolate cake. This is normal. The cake it still more than halfway to the top. *These are pretty rich and sweet, and make a really great dessert for two people to share with two spoons… but can still be eaten by one. *You can use other sizes of jars, just use your eye to decided how much batter to use, never going over half way. If you have too much batter, just make a few cupcakes.
